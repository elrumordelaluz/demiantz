Synopsis_

Like every morning, the alarm clock rings on the bedside table, on it a black and white image of an older woman, the house is dark. Anton (74), a veteran immigrant from the Soviet Union, walks to the window and opens the blinds, loud street noises, Anton looks out for a few moments and shuts the window. At the evening he sits in front of the TV but there’s no program that keeps him awake.

One morning, Anton sits on a street bench and after a few moments comes Anna (78), a veteran immigrant from Argentina. She claims that her radio is broken and asks whether he can examine it. Anton has little hesitation but he didn’t refuse. He examines the radio. The next day, a knock on the door and Anton discovers tense Anna, he invites her to enter. She walks into the house and immediately goes to the photos, Anton slightly stuttering and tells her that the woman in the picture named Esther, his body trembling slightly. That evening, Anton disassemble the radio but won’t touch it. Betrayal Esther’s memory overwhelms him and does not allow him to move onward.

The next morning he sits again on the bench. Anna awaits for him with a big smile, a red shirt and black pants. She holds a plastic box and offers him a taste of the pie she had prepared. When he takes a bite, his face almost smiled fill with longing. They’re eyes meet. That evening Anton fixes the radio and enjoy the hoarse sounds the old radio plays.

In the morning he got up before the alarm clock, working out in front of the mirror and goes to the bench. He wears a red button-down shirt and blue jeans, bag with radio standing there. Time passes, and Anna does not reach. He goes to her apartment and knocks at the door, the door opens. Anna smiles, happy that her radio is fixed she invites Anton to enter, he goes in. Very slowly, he goes inside and close the door behind him.

Director's statement

In the last couple of years I got to spend a lot of time with my grandmother. Ever since my grandfather died in 2009, I discovered a different woman. A woman who doesn’t dwell on the end, but lives every day in the happiest, most fulfilled way. That led her to meet an older man, like her, who helps her spend her days laughing and talking about today’s global reality.

My grandfather stayed in her memory, regardless of her changing, and every day I ask myself how he would’ve dealt with this situation. And perhaps due to his absence, I chose to make a movie about an older man in such a situation, in which his wife passed and he must deal with the loneliness. Such is the character of Anton, only unlike my grandmother, Anton doesn’t believe one can start over. As far as he’s concerned his story is over, and he’s just waiting for the end.

By his side is Anna, his next door neighbour, who, despite her age, still tried to enjoy the moment, and tried to lead Anton into thinking better of the future. Her actions lead him to appreciate time more, think of the now, and maybe even believe you can turn the leaf and start a new story.

Like my grandfather, I fear new beginnings. It always seems like a roadblock to be, one that tests me to see how ready I am for what’s to come. That’s my main motivation in making this movie, to give myself to the character of Anton, try to understand him and through him understand myself. I’m interested in the relationship between Anton and me, as well as Anton and Anna. I think that this movie can offer a new perspective. Sometimes we look at the life of elders as something defined and set, they’re set to the side-lines and don’t get much of our attention. The story between Anna and Anton is a love story, a simple story of the lonely lives of two elders, dealing with the lives of widows very differently. I’m trying to tell a love story that could live outside the frame.

Our photography style will consist of long, static shots, through which the simple and quiet routine between the two neighbours will shine. The story is told from Anton’s point of view, so the camera will reveal the things he looks at, and the things he wishes he could look at. Anton has a lot of fears and memories he can’t articulate, so we will try to emote them through filming and soundtrack; the feelings of loneliness and helplessness, and everything that he goes through since he was left alive and alone.

Demian Tzatzkin
